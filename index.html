Dr. Gladys Mae West (1930- )

Dr. Gladys Mae West is an American mathematician known for her contributions to the mathematical modeling of the shape of the Earth and her work on the development of the satellite geodesy models that were eventually incorporated into the Global Positioning System (GPS).

Not only is she a math wizard, she is also a style icon! Dr. West sported this look at her US Airforce Space and Missile Pioneers Hall of Fame induction ceremony in 2018.

Please enjoy this Timeline of Dr. Mae West's life thus far, highlighting just a few of her many accomplishments:

  • 1930: Gladys Mae West was born into a farming family in Sutherland, Virginia. Growing up, she always knew she wasn't build for that struffle lifestyle, working from sun-up to sundown in the heat, so she learned to prioritize her studies early on.
  • 1948: She graduated at the top of her high school class and went on to go to Virginia State Univeristy (VSU) on a full scholarship, majoring in Mathematics.
  • 1952: Ms. West graduated from college.
  • 1955: She graduated with her Master's in Mathematics from VSU.
  • 1956: Gladys was hired to work for the US Naval Surface Warfare Center, becoming the second black woman ever hired and one of 4 black employees at the facility.
  • 1960s: She was part of a project that created the first satellite that could remotely sense oceans.
  • 1970s-1980s: Dr. West programmed an IBM supercomputer to deliver calculations that modeled the shape of the earth. This data was eventually used as the basis for the GPS systems we use today!
  • 1998: She retired from her job at the Naval Base after 42 years.
  • 2018: Gladys Mae West was inducted into the US Airforce Space and Missile Pioneers Hall of Fame.

To learn more about Ms. West, check out her autobiography, It Began With a Dream , at your local library!
